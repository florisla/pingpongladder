Rules

Ladder ranking

  • Initially the players are ranked randomly, with only some of the best placed intentionally at the bottom.
  • A player can challenge an opponent who is 1 to 3 places above him.
  • A challenge is official once it is announced in the shoutbox.
  • You cannot deny a challenge; this is equal to defeating three times with 11-0.
  • You cannot challenge the last player you played against (either as challenger or challengee)
  • If you have received multiple challenges, you have to play them in order (see shoutbox). Since a match result can influence the ranking, subsequent challenges may become invalid.
  • It the lower-ranked challenger wins, both players swap places in the ranking; so naturally, the best players are rising to the top.

Absence

  • If you're not in, you don't have to play -- being out of office is therefor not a problem.
  • But players who have played almost no games (amount to be decided) are considered to not have participated in the final ranking.

End of tournament

  • The tournament will take several weeks.
  • The end date of the tournament is still to be decided and will be communicated well in advance.

Matches

  • The match winner is the one who first wins two sets of 11 points (so max three sets).
  • You can only win a set with a minimal difference of 2 points.
  • Regular rules apply, see here.

Serves

  • Serves are switched every 2 points.
  • As soon as both players have 10 points, serves are switched every point.
  • Serves do not have to cross play-halves.
  • A serve which touches the net but bounces correctly, needs to be played again.
  • There is no second serve; a serve which which bounces incorrectly is a lost point.

Whining

  • Excessive whining is punished by your removal from the tournament.
  • Excessive is defined as claiming >10 times that you're "not sure about participating", "ready to quit", "no longer feel like it", "don't see the point", and the like.
