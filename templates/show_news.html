Tournament has started

As of 2015-08-04 the tabletennis tournament has started.

The tournament formula is not a poules + knock out phase, but rather a ladder ranking.

Check the rules; and see the current ranking.

How to go on from here?

  1. Look at the ranking
  2. Pick an opponent who is 1, 2 or 3 positions higher on the ranking than you
  3. Play that opponent
  4. Log in to the tournament website (password: pingpong) and log the game score
  5. If you've won, you receive your opponents rank spot and vice versa
  6. Repeat

Tournament formula: ladder competition

In order to let anybody play as many games as they like, and to avoid people having to leave the tournament too early, we won't be using a poules/knockout tournament formula, but a ladder competiton.

It goes like this:

  • Players are ranked, from worse (at the top) to the best (at the bottom)
  • A player can challenge an opponent who is 1 to 3 places above him
  • It the lower-ranked challenger wins, both players swap places in the ranking; so naturally, the best players are rising to the top
  • You cannot deny a challenge; this is equal to defeating

More rules will be described on the rules page.

Tournament formula to be decided

Deciding on a tournament formula is not easy if the number of players is not a power of two.

If you have 16 players it's easy: 2 poules of 8 provide a ranking, and 8 matches have 8 winners which is then reduced to 4, 2, 1.

With 14 players you still want to end up with 8 or 4 players to start the to 2 and 1, but there are not enough players to hold 8 matches to start with.

One solution is to hold only 4 matches and remove the bottom ranked players from each poule from the tournament straight away. This is not much fun for the affected players.

The nicer solution is to hold 6 matches, and give the top ranked players from each poule a wilcard to the next round. This has the benefit that the lesser players are saved from the risk of encountering these 'dangerous' adversaries in the first knock-out round.
